CapDiT: A Caption-Guided Diffusion Transformer for Zero-Shot Text-to-Speech with Expressive Paralinguistic Vocalizations

Abstract

Current zero-shot text-to-speech (TTS) systems excel at voice cloning but largely neglect paralinguistic vocalizations such as laughter, sighs, and interjections, which are essential for natural and expressive speech. Existing approaches suffer from an inherent trade-off between precise positional control via categorical tokens and fine-grained expressive rendering through natural language captions. In this paper, we propose CapDiT, a caption-guided diffusion transformer framework that reconciles this trade-off through a dual-conditioning mechanism. Our approach introduces three key innovations: (1) a refinement pipeline that transforms coarse categorical labels from closed-vocabulary datasets into rich, open-vocabulary captions using a large multimodal language model; (2) a cross-attention DiT architecture that integrates caption embeddings to guide the diffusion process for nuanced expressive synthesis while preserving positional control via special tokens; and (3) a Decoupled Classifier-Free Guidance mechanism that independently balances timbre fidelity and expressive adherence. We integrate CapDiT into two representative zero-shot TTS backbones—F5-TTS and CosyVoice 3—and demonstrate consistent improvements across both. Our framework establishes a broadly applicable solution for expressive speech synthesis with fine-grained control over paralinguistic vocalizations.
